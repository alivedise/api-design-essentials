# [ADP-46] API Guideline Exception

Each team or business unit may have specific concerns or unique requirements when designing APIs. The role of the ADP (API Design Principles) guidance is to assist and support every API designer in creating effective and consistent APIs, rather than to act as a strict enforcer of rules. The ADP guidance serves as a helpful resource, offering best practices and recommendations to facilitate the design process, but it is not intended to be rigidly prescriptive.

It is recognized that there can be valid reasons for deviating slightly from established conventions, particularly in APIs with a designated audience of `COMPONENT_INTERNAL`. These APIs are intended for internal use within a specific component or system, and as such, may have unique constraints or requirements that necessitate minor adjustments to the standard guidelines. Therefore, it is considered reasonable to allow small deviations from the conventions in these cases, as long as they do not compromise the overall integrity, maintainability, or interoperability of the API. This flexibility is intended to enable teams to address their specific needs while still adhering to the core principles of good API design.
